National Urban Health Care Mission

The objective of the National Urban Health Care Mission (NUHM) is to improve the healthcare delivery system in urban areas of India. The mission aims to provide accessible, affordable, and quality healthcare services to the urban population, particularly the vulnerable sections and slum dwellers. It focuses on strengthening the primary healthcare infrastructure, including urban health centers and dispensaries, and enhancing the capacity of healthcare providers. NUHM also promotes preventive and promotive healthcare measures, including immunization, maternal and child health services, and disease control programs. The mission seeks to ensure equitable healthcare access and reduce health disparities in urban areas, ultimately improving the overall health and well-being of urban residents.

    • Mukhyamantri Amrutum Yojana Scheme

    The objective of the Mukhyamantri Amrutum Yojana scheme is to provide affordable and quality healthcare to the economically vulnerable population in India. The scheme aims to address the financial burden associated with medical treatments by offering cashless treatment and coverage for hospitalization expenses, surgeries, and selected medical procedures. It primarily targets families living below the poverty line and ensures access to essential healthcare services without compromising their financial stability. By offering comprehensive health coverage, the scheme aims to improve healthcare outcomes, reduce mortality rates, and enhance the overall well-being of the beneficiaries, thereby promoting inclusivity and social welfare.

    • Mukhya Mantri Himachal Health Care Scheme (himcare)

    The HIMCARE scheme seeks to ensure that every individual in Himachal Pradesh has access to affordable and quality healthcare services. It aims to provide financial protection to vulnerable sections of society by covering medical expenses for various illnesses and treatments. The scheme promotes inclusivity by targeting families who fall under the socioeconomic vulnerable category, with special focus on the marginalized and underprivileged. Through this initiative, the government intends to reduce the financial burden on individuals and families, promote preventive healthcare practices, and improve the overall health outcomes and well-being of the people in Himachal Pradesh.

    Eligibility Criteria :

    For :BPL families, senior citizens, MANREGA Workers, Registered vendors, children in orphanages, window or dovorced woman, Disabled People, ASHA workers
    Gender : Both
    State : Himachal Pradesh

    Required Documents:

  • Category: BPL
    Copy of BPL certificate attested by the Panchayat Secretary within the previous one month.
  • Category: Registered Street Vendors
    Registration Certificate attested by the Executive Officer, MC/NP/NAC within the previous one month.
  • Category: MNREGA Worker
    MNREGA Job Card and Online MIS Report indicating a minimum of 50 days of work under MNREGA in the previous or current financial year. These documents should be duly attested by the concerned Panchayat Secretary/BDO.
  • Category: Ekal Naaris
    Certificate to be issued by the Child Development Program Officer (CDPO) of the concerned area. The certificate should include details of widows, divorced, legally separated, or unmarried women above 40 years of age.
  • Category: Disabled >40%
    Medical Disability Certificate showing permanent disability.
  • Category: Senior Citizens above 70 years of age
    Any valid age proof document.
  • Category: Anganwari Workers/Helpers
    Certificate from the Child Development Program Officer (CDPO) of the concerned area.
  • Category: ASHA Workers
    Certificate from the Block Medical Officer (BMO) of the concerned area.
  • Category: Mid-Day Meal Workers
    Certificate from the Block Elementary Education Officer of the concerned area.

    • Rashtriya Swasthya Bima Yojana

    The objective of the Rashtriya Swasthya Bima Yojana (RSBY) is to provide financial protection and access to quality healthcare services for the underprivileged population of India. The scheme aims to address the healthcare needs of the poor by providing them with a smart card-based health insurance cover. Through RSBY, beneficiaries can avail themselves of cashless treatment at empaneled hospitals and clinics, ensuring that they receive timely medical care without facing financial hardships. The scheme focuses on promoting social inclusiveness, reducing out-of-pocket healthcare expenses, and improving the overall health and well-being of the vulnerable sections of society, thereby contributing to the larger goal of achieving universal healthcare coverage in India.

    Eligibility Criteria :

    For : The members of a family that are listed in the Below Poverty Line list created by the State government can enrol in this scheme.
    Gender : Both
    State : All State
    Cast : For all

    Required Documents:

  • Ration Card
  • Income Certificate
  • Aadhaar card
  • Address proof
  • Passport size photograph
  • BPL Certificate
  • Mobile Number, etc.
