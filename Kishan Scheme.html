Gramin Bhandaran Yojana

The objective of the Gramin Bhandaran Yojana is to provide financial assistance and support to farmers in rural areas of India for the construction and modernization of rural storage infrastructure. The scheme aims to minimize post-harvest losses by creating adequate storage facilities such as warehouses, cold storage, and other storage infrastructure at the village level. This initiative is designed to improve the agricultural marketing system by ensuring the availability of proper storage facilities for farmers, reducing wastage, and enhancing their income. By promoting the development of rural storage infrastructure, the Gramin Bhandaran Yojana contributes to the overall growth and development of the agricultural sector in rural India.

    • Pradhan Mantri Kisan Maan Dhan Yojana (PMKMDY)

    The PMKMDY aims to provide a social security net for farmers by encouraging them to save for their retirement. Under this scheme, eligible farmers between the ages of 18 and 40 are encouraged to contribute a certain amount of money each month. The government matches this contribution, which creates a pension fund for the farmers. Upon reaching the age of 60, the farmers become eligible to receive a monthly pension, thereby ensuring financial stability during their old age. The scheme aims to address the issue of farmer distress and improve their overall welfare.

    • Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana

    The objective of the Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) is to provide financial protection to farmers against crop loss or damage due to natural calamities, pests, and diseases. The scheme aims to stabilize farmers' income and ensure food security by providing affordable insurance coverage for yield losses. It encourages farmers to adopt modern agricultural practices and technologies by reducing the risks associated with farming. PMFBY also aims to create a transparent and efficient agricultural insurance system by leveraging technology for faster claim settlements. Ultimately, the objective is to safeguard the interests of farmers and promote sustainable agriculture in India.

    • Kisan Credit Card

    The objective of the Kisan Credit Card (KCC) is to provide financial assistance to farmers for their agricultural needs in a convenient and affordable manner. It aims to enhance the agricultural sector's productivity and ensure the financial inclusion of farmers. The KCC facilitates timely and adequate credit to farmers for crop cultivation, post-harvest expenses, marketing activities, and other agricultural requirements. It helps farmers access formal credit sources easily, eliminating their dependence on informal and exploitative lenders. The KCC also promotes the adoption of modern farming techniques, purchase of high-quality inputs, and investment in farm machinery and equipment. Overall, the KCC plays a crucial role in improving farmers' livelihoods and sustaining agricultural development.
